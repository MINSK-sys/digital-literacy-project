# 📱 Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

As a Student Digital Ambassador, I have compiled this guide to help my peers maintain a professional and safe digital footprint.

### ✅ The Do's (Best Practices)
* **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Always use an extra layer of security on your social accounts to prevent unauthorized access.
* **Use a Professional Profile Picture:** Ensure your LinkedIn and other professional handles use a clear, appropriate headshot.
* **Fact-Check Before Sharing:** Verify the authenticity of news or campus alerts before reposting to prevent the spread of misinformation.
* **Privacy Settings Audit:** Regularly check your privacy settings to control who can see your personal posts and tagged photos.
* **Network Purposefully:** Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni and industry professionals in your field of study.

### ❌ The Don'ts (Avoid These)
* **Don't Post Sensitive Info:** Never share your registration number, OTPs, or home address on public stories or posts.
* **Don't Engage in Cyberbullying:** Avoid negative arguments or "trolling," as digital footprints are permanent and can be seen by future employers.
* **Don't Overshare Real-Time Location:** Avoid "checking in" at exact locations while you are still there to ensure personal physical safety.
* **Don't Click Suspicious Links:** Ignore "free giveaway" or "urgent prize" links in DMs, as these are often phishing attempts.
* **Don't Post Disparaging Content:** Avoid posting content that speaks ill of your institution, professors, or peers, as it reflects poorly on your professionalism.
