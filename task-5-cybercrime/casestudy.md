# Cybercrime Case Study - UPI/Online Payment Fraud

### Description of the Crime
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of financial cybercrime that exploits the "request money" feature of payment apps.In this scenario, a scammer contacts a student claiming they accidentally transferred money to the student's account and requests a "refund".

### Step-by-Step Execution
1. **The Hook:** The attacker sends a "Collect Request" to the victim's UPI ID or mobile number.
2. **The Deception:** The attacker calls the victim, creating a sense of urgency and claiming they are a distressed individual who made a mistake.
3. **The Trap:** The victim is told to "Click the link and enter the PIN" to approve the refund.
4. **The Theft:** Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, they are actually authorizing a payment *out* of their account rather than receiving one.

### Targets and Consequences
College students are frequent targets because they often use UPI for quick peer-to-peer transactions.The consequences include immediate loss of funds, potential identity theft if bank details are further compromised, and significant mental stress. It is vital to remember: A PIN is only required to *send* money, never to *receive* it.
